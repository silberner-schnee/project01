Mini-biography

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was the scientist credited for creating the AC (alternating current) electric system commonly used in today's world, and for designing Tesla coils (electrical resonant transformer circuits), which are still in use today in radio technology.
Although Tesla designed and developed many other important technologies, they have been attributed to other inventors (George Westinghouse owned the patent rights for AC technology).


Tesla greatly contributed to the discovery of the rotating magnetic field, radar technology, electric generators, the induction motor, and x-ray technology.
Sadly, despite his many accomplishments Tesla was never a rich man, and due to his lack of financial prowess many of his ideas have been relegated to notebooks where they remain.

Timeline of important events

  • 10th July, 1859

    Birth

    Born at midnight during a storm in Smiljan, Serbia. Nikola Tesla was the 4th child in a family consisting of 5 children.

  • 1870

    Illness

    Shortly after completing High School, Tesla contracted cholera. He was hospitalized for 1 year nearly facing death before recovering.

  • 1875

    College Enrollment

    Tesla enrolled into a Polytechnic academic institution under a military scholarship. He achieved the highest grades possible and managed to found a culture club.
    In his second year at the college, he was already arguing with his professors, this time over the functionality of the Gramme dynamo.

  • 1880

    Pursuit of Philosophy

    With financial help from his uncle, Tesla relocated to Prague to complete his studies.
    Because he did not speak Czech or Greek, he failed to qualify for the courses he wished to pursue, finally deciding to study Philosophy.
    Tesla did not receive a grade for any of these courses.

  • 1881

    Early Career

    Moving to Budapest, Hungary, Tesla intitally tried to apply to work for a telegraph company called "Budapest Telegraph Exchange" (BTS).
    The company was not currently in operation, so he took up a draftsman position in another company.
    Within a few months, BTS had resumed business, and Tesla successfully applied for a position there as a Chief Electrical Engineer.
    Tesla was responsible for improving the company's performance during his tenture there.

  • 1882

    Working for Edison

    A year later, Tesla found himself in Paris, France working with the Continental Edison Company
    Tesla began there working on indoor lighting systems.
    At this company Tesla improved his engineering knowledge
    The local management noticed his ability and added him to the team responsible for producing dynamos and motors.

  • 1885

    First Patents

    Through help from Benjamin Vail and Robert Lane, Tesla received funding for his light production company.
    Tesla aquired several patents, including one for his enchanced DC motor.

  • 1887

    Reimagining the Electric Motor

    Tesla developed an induction motor that was operated by AC
    This made it possible for long-distance power transmission in the USA & EU.

  • 1890

    Wireless Power Transmission

    Tesla experimented with different approaches to transmit power by inductive and capacitive coupling.
    This was facilitated through AC voltages produced by his Tesla coil.

  • 1891

    Tesla Coil Patent

    Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the USA and patent his design for the Tesla coil.

  • 1943

    Death

    At the age of 86 in a hotel room in New York, Tesla passed away.
    The cause of death was determined to be from coronary complications (heart problems).
    Within a few days the FBI ordered for the aquisition of his belongings.
